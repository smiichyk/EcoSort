Guide to Proper Waste Sorting

Learn how to dispose of waste responsibly with this straightforward guide to Irelandâ€™s waste sorting system. Discover which items belong in each bin to make a positive environmental impact, reduce landfill waste, and support a cleaner community.

Sorting waste properly ensures that materials are correctly processed, reduces the amount sent to landfills, and helps protect the environment.

Always check your local waste collection guidelines, as specific rules and accepted items may vary.

General Waste Bin (Black)

What to Throw: Items that cannot be recycled or composted, such as:

  • Non-recyclable plastics (like plastic bags, wrappers, and film)
  • Dirty food containers (like pizza boxes soaked with grease)
  • Broken ceramics or glass
  • Hygiene products (like tissues, nappies, or sanitary items)

What to Avoid: Avoid putting recyclables, compostable items, hazardous materials (like batteries, paint, and electronics), or bulky items like furniture.

Recycling Bin (Green)

What to Throw: Clean and dry items that can be recycled, such as:

  • Plastic bottles, containers, and lids (usually labeled with a recycling symbol)
  • Paper products (newspapers, magazines, and cardboard boxes)
  • Metal cans and tins (like soda cans and food cans)
  • Glass jars and bottles (if local guidelines permit glass in the recycling bin)

What to Avoid: Do not put food waste, dirty containers, plastic bags, or items like batteries, electronics, and polystyrene foam (unless they are recyclable in your area).

Organic Waste Bin (Brown)

What to Throw: Organic materials that will decompose, including:

  • Food scraps (fruit peels, vegetable trimmings, coffee grounds, and eggshells)
  • Yard waste (like leaves, small branches, and grass clippings)
  • Paper towels and napkins (only if they are not contaminated with chemicals or oils)
  • Biodegradable food packaging (if certified compostable)

What to Avoid: Avoid placing plastic, glass, metals, pet waste, or non-compostable packaging in this bin, as they can disrupt the composting process.

Glass Bin

What to Throw: Only clean glass items, such as:

  • Glass bottles (wine, soda, and juice bottles)
  • Glass jars (jars for sauces, and jams)

What to Avoid: Do not throw ceramic items, mirrors, light bulbs, or heat-resistant glass (like Pyrex), as these require special recycling methods.
