Recycling Symbols and Their Meanings

Learn what each symbol means to recycle effectively and responsibly. Understanding these symbols helps you make informed decisions for sustainable waste management.

The Mobius Loop

The Green Dot

This trademark means that the manufacturer or supplier has contributed to packaging recycling efforts in Europe. However, this symbol does not necessarily mean that the packaging is recyclable, has been recycled, or will be recycled.

Plastic Recycling

These symbols specify the type of plastic used in the item. All plastics can be placed in household recycling bins regardless of the code or color. Find out more...

Non-Household Waste

Indicates that the item, usually an electrical product, should not be disposed of in a household bin but must be recycled at designated collection points like WEEE facilities or electrical stores.

Glass Recycling

Indicates that glass containers, such as bottles and jars, should be recycled. It's recommended to rinse glass items and drop them off at a local bottle bank.

Steel Recycling

Indicates that the product is made of steel and can be placed in household recycling bins, provided they are clean and dry.

Aluminium Recycling

This symbol indicates that the product is made from recyclable aluminium and can be disposed of in household recycling bins. Aluminium cans are also commonly accepted at local bottle banks.

Compostable

This symbol confirms that the product is certified as industrially compostable according to the European standard. Compostable packaging should not be mixed with recyclable items.

Tidyman

This symbol encourages consumers to dispose of waste thoughtfully and use a bin to help keep the environment clean. It does not indicate recyclability or compostability.

Widely Recycled

Symbol shows that the packaging can be recycled through household recycling.

Partially Recyclable

This symbol indicates that some parts of the packaging can be recycled while others need to be disposed of as general waste.

Not Yet Recycled

Indicates that the item is currently non-recyclable.

Plastic Recycling Codes

Plastics are categorized by specific codes

that help identify their type and recyclability:

1: PETE (Polyethylene Terephthalate)

Commonly used for water and soda bottles, PETE is widely recyclable and often turned into new bottles or fabrics. Look for recycling centers that accept it.

2: HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)

Often used in milk jugs, detergent bottles, and household products, HDPE is one of the easiest plastics to recycle and is usually accepted by most recycling facilities.

3: V (Polyvinyl Chloride)

Common in plumbing pipes and some toys, PVC is less commonly recycled and can be challenging to process due to its chlorine content. It is often not accepted in curbside recycling.

4: LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene)

Used for plastic bags and some containers, LDPE is recyclable, but not all recycling centers accept it. Check with local facilities.

5: PP (Polypropylene)

Found in yogurt containers, straws, and bottle caps, PP is becoming more widely recyclable and is known for its strength and flexibility.

6: PS (Polystyrene)

Often used in disposable coffee cups and Styrofoam packaging, PS is challenging to recycle due to its light weight and brittle nature, though some specialized facilities may accept it.

7: OTHER (Various Plastics)

This category includes various types like acrylic and nylon. These plastics are rarely recycled and are commonly found in multi-material products.
